Lagvinari is a celebrated Georgian wine brand recognized both in Georgia and throughout the world, with exports reaching over 20 countries. But it is more than just a wine brand. Now Lagvinari is the embodiment of Georgian tradition, the passion of creator, Doctor Eko Glonti, the soul of the Caucasus wine region and a true expression of the land.

Founder Eko's lifelong love of wine led him to winemaking. His inaugural release in 2011 marked the start of a journey where each vintage blends bold experimentation – in fermentation, aging, and diverse grape selections – with profound respect for Georgian tradition. Crafted in georgian qvevri with minimal intervention and low sulfites, Lagvinari wines consistently deliver exceptional quality.

The only thing that always remains unchanged is the highest quality of his wines and the delight they evoke in fans. We would say that Doctor Glonti's wines are ‘Georgian Burgundy’, reflecting their complexity, depth, and esteemed character.

Taste the difference of Lagvinari.

The main value of the cellar "39 Qvevri" is a traditional Georgian wine created from local grape varieties and aged in the old way in Kvevri. The winery is located in the village of Bitsmendi, in one of the most significant historical places of Kartli, the center of viticulture and winemaking. There is also a winery's own vineyard, where four Georgian grape varieties are grown (Shavkapito, Goruli Mtsvane, Kisi and Hihvi).

Bitsmendi has a special historical significance and is associated with the names of famous traditional Georgian winemakers, such as Guramishvili, with his famous vineyards and cellars, or Ilya Tsinamdzgvrishvili, one of the most prominent figures of the XIX century, the founder and teacher of the Tsinamdzgvriantkar Agricultural School.

Location: Mtskheta Municipality, Kartli region, Georgia

Annual production: 60 000 bottles

The wine cellar was established in 2017, on the site of an old wine cellar, there was an 80-year-old vineyard, which was renovated, in the same space, there is a yard and a residential house. The Cellar and I both have the name of my Grandfather  —  Levan. We mainly buy bio-certified grapes in the Kakheti region, from which we make natural wines. The first Qvevri wine was made in 2018 and bottled in 2019. The cellar has 18-ton Qvevris. While operating at full capacity, it is possible to produce 10,000 bottles of Qvevri wine annually.

We produce wine using the traditional Kakhetian method of winemaking: macerating with skins and stalks for 6 months in Qvevri and then aging 5 months without skins and stalks. This way, prolonged maceration, and natural filtration happen. Wines are a live and natural, without any yeasts and additives. Wines are aged in Qvevri, at the natural temperature of 14° and after bottling, in the wine cellar 16°. We produce 4 types of wine. Red dry — Saperavi, Amber (white) dry — Rkatsiteli, Kakhetian Green, and Kisi. The name of the wine and cellar is "Levani's Marani Akhmeta".

Location: 77 Chavchavadze str, Akhmeta, Kakheti Region, Georgia

Annual production: 8 000 bottles

The history of "Anbani Wine" began with the friendship of four guys Mamuka, Amiran, George and Giorgi. The guys, like true Georgians, decided to follow the path of their ancestors and engage in wine production using the ancient Georgian traditional approach, especially since they had a common hobby — natural winemaking. It was in 2015. For almost a year, the founders of the winery were looking for a Vineyard, on the land of which they felt the connection with the vine, which gave hope for prosperity.

Anbani now owns a vineyard that was planted in 1970. Soon after, the Marani of Kvevri was built, and the founders realized their dream - their natural wine is now produced in the same way as their ancestors did for a long time.

The David Leladze's Family Winery, one of the oldest in the Imeteri region, has been cultivating and processing grapes for more than a hundred years.

Already 4 generations of this family, since 1900, have kept the traditions of winemaking, continuing the work of their ancestors. David and his family take care of the vineyards on their own and carefully bring the grapes to the state of wine, as ancient traditions dictate. The terroirs of the Imereti wine region, in which this winery is located, are famous for their fertile soil and favorable climate for organic and healthy grape growth. David is particularly proud of the fact that he makes sure that his vines are grown organically - he uses only biological products and avoids any harmful chemical fertilizers. When working in the cellar, the winemaker uses pitchers and a press made of hazelnut wood from 1900, just like his great-grandfather.

The family winery of David Leladze is a small, but conscientious enterprise, whose team puts quality above quantity. They produce only 7,000 bottles of wine a year. It is noteworthy that their Dry Pink wine Aladasturi received a silver medal at the International Rose Championship in 2021.

Location: Baghdati District Village Rokhi, 2nd Street, House N19, Imereti Region, Georgia

Annual production: 7 000 bottles
