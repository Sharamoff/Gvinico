Aleksandrouli a grape variety indigenous to the Racha region of Georgia, holds a significant presence as the most widely cultivated grape in this area. It plays a pivotal role as one half of the iconic semi-sweet wine Khvanchkara, which is celebrated for its rich history and unique flavor profile. This grape thrives in the microclimatic conditions of Racha, contributing to the region's viticultural heritage and the production of distinctive Georgian wines renowned both locally and internationally.

Name

Aleksandrouli (ალექსანდროული in Georgian) has undergone several name changes throughout its history, but its current name is believed to have originated from the dedication to the royal figure Aleksandre, who played a pioneering role in popularizing the semi-sweet style of this variety.

Viticulture

Aleksandrouli, despite its popularity, is known for its relatively low-yielding nature. It typically forms medium-sized bunches containing berries of medium size, ranging from round to sometimes oval in shape. The grape demonstrates a moderate resistance to mildew, making it relatively resilient in vineyard management practices. While it can thrive in various soil types, Aleksandrouli reaches its full potential on calcareous and clay-rich soils, where it produces wines of exceptional quality.

Wine styles

The ability of this variety to accumulate high amounts of sugars in specific terroirs provides winemakers with versatility to explore different styles.

  • Semi-Sweet Red Wine Khvanchkara is an iconic wine crafted from two grape varieties, Aleksandrouli and Mujuretuli. This natural semi-sweet wine holds Protected Designation of Origin status and is renowned for its unique character and rich history.

  • Dry Red Wine can be made as a monovarietal or blended with its iconic sibling, Mujuretuli. Dry wines from this variety also show extreme potential when aged in oak, gaining a totally new dimension and complexity.
