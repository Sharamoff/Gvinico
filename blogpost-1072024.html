Introducing Winemaking Infographics

Hey wine lovers! We’re super excited to roll out our newest feature: Winemaking Infographics. These cool visuals show off the main winemaking processes like fermentation and aging for every wine on our site. Now, you can see exactly how your favorite wines go from grape to bottle!

Why Infographics?

Wine isn’t just a drink; it’s a story of tradition and craftsmanship. At festivals and tastings, winemakers spend a lot of time explaining their magic. Our Winemaking Infographics make these stories easy to see and fun to understand.

Introducing Winemaking Infographics

Every wine diagram will show:

  • Vessel Type: From Qvevri to stainless steel tanks and oak barrels.
  • Skins/Stems: How much grape skin and stem are used, impacting flavor and complexity.
  • Lees: The use of lees (those funky dead yeast cells) for adding richness.
  • Duration: A timeline of the wine’s journey.
  • Temperature Levels: The specific temps during fermentation and aging.
Traditional Kakhetian method with full skins and stems, extended with 3 years of no skin aging, used for winemaking of Qvevriseuli Rkatsiteli.

Colors

The transformation from juice to wine encompasses a spectrum of colors, including white, red, and the delightful rosé. Additionally, Georgia's famed amber wines, often referred to as liquid gold, exhibit varying shades based on the extent of skin and stem contact used during fermentation.

Saperavi qvevri skin contact with oak aging, used for winemaking of ORILO Saperavi Khashmi Oak.
